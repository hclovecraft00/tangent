I wanted to share something I'm involved with that might be worth your attention — and ask for your help with a few things.

Tangent is a 24-month fellowship providing up to S$200,000 for young people in Singapore — aged 18–21, or 18–22 for men with NS obligations — who are self-directed and motivated but whose potential doesn't show up in grades or credentials. They're the ones Singapore's existing incubators and accelerators structurally can't reach.

The fellowship begins with a four-week programme: S$1,000, AI tools, and human guides. No mandatory courses, no assignments. Participants define their own work and show results at the end of four weeks. Those who want to continue pitch for the full 24-month fellowship. The fellowship is also open to anyone who wants to pitch, whether or not they've done the four weeks.

If you'd be willing to help in any of these ways:
- Refer a young person who belongs in Tangent
- Come in as a guide for two hours during a cohort — available to participants, not lecturing
- Support the programme with resources

tangent.org.sg has details on the programme and how it works, how to apply, how to refer someone, and how to get involved as a guide.
