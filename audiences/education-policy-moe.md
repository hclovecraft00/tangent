I wanted to share a programme I'm running and ask for your help with a few things.

Tangent is a 24-month fellowship providing up to S$200,000 for young people in Singapore aged 18–21 (or 18–22 for men with NS obligations), designed to identify and develop unconventional entrepreneurial talent upstream of Singapore's existing incubator and accelerator ecosystem. Tangent is privately funded and doesn't report to a government ministry — which is part of why it can reach people that institutional programmes structurally cannot.

The fellowship begins with a four-week programme: S$1,000, AI tools, and human guides. No mandatory courses, no assessments. Participants define their own work and show results at the end of four weeks. Several universities have already agreed to defer matriculation for participants, allowing them to treat the four weeks as a low-cost, time-limited experiment. Those who want to continue pitch for the full 24-month fellowship.

Three things I'd welcome your help with:
- Referring strong candidates directly to us
- Connecting us with teachers and school-level referrers who can nominate students
- Discussing whether there are policy channels that could support this kind of programme

tangent.org.sg has details on the programme and how it works, how to apply, how to refer someone, and how to get involved as a guide.
