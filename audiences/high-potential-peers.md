Tangent is a 24-month fellowship for young people in Singapore who want to build something on their own terms — a business, a social enterprise, a not-for-profit, or anything that could eventually make them a good living. Fellows receive up to S$200,000 over two years.

It starts with a four-week programme: S$1,000, AI tools, and human guides. No mandatory courses, no assignments. You define the work, show us what you found at the end of four weeks, and your commitment ends there if you want it to. If you want to continue, you pitch for the full fellowship.

No required grades, no polished pitch. Age range 18–21 (or 18–22 for men with NS obligations).

If this sounds like you, or like someone you know — tangent.org.sg has details on the programme and how it works, how to apply, how to refer someone, and how to get involved as a guide.
