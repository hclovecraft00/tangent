I wanted to tell you about something I'm building and ask for your help.

Tangent is a 24-month fellowship providing up to S$200,000 for young people in Singapore — aged 18–21 for women and men without NS obligations, 18–22 for men with NS obligations — who are self-directed and motivated but whose potential isn't legible to Singapore's existing system. They wouldn't apply to Antler or NUS Enterprise, and those programmes wouldn't select them if they did.

The fellowship begins with a four-week programme: S$1,000, AI tools, and human guides. No courses, no assignments. Participants define their own work and show results at the end of four weeks. Several universities have already agreed to defer matriculation, so participants can treat it as a low-cost experiment. Those who want to continue pitch for the full 24-month fellowship.

Three things I'd love your help with:
- Come in as a guide for two hours during a cohort — available to participants if they want to talk, nothing structured required
- Refer anyone you know who belongs in this programme
- Share this with others in your network who should know about it

tangent.org.sg has details on the programme and how it works, how to apply, how to refer someone, and how to get involved as a guide.
