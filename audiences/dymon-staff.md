Subject: Tangent — a programme we're hosting on the 10th floor

Hi all,

We'll be hosting a small group of young people from Tangent on the 10th floor, and I wanted to give you a quick sense of what it is and what to expect.

Tangent is a programme for Singapore-based young people — roughly 18 to 22 — who want to build something on their own terms: a business, a social enterprise, a not-for-profit, or any other idea that could eventually make them a good living. The people Tangent is looking for are unconventional in the sense that they wouldn't show up on the radar of the usual incubators and accelerators. Capable and motivated, but not the kind of person the conventional system is built to see.

The way it works: a small cohort of around 10 participants comes in during workdays for four weeks. There's no curriculum and no assignments. They work on their own ideas — figuring out what to do, asking for help when they need it, and showing the programme what they found at the end of four weeks. A handful of guides, people from different backgrounds who've agreed to be available, will come by during the programme to spend time with participants. At the end of the four weeks, a subset of participants may continue into a longer programme providing up to S$200,000 per participant over 24 months to develop their idea and bring it to market.

If you're curious, please feel free to come by and spend some time with them — you'd be welcome. And if you'd like to be a guide yourself — which just means being available for a couple of hours during the programme, not teaching or advising — email guide@tangent.org.sg.
